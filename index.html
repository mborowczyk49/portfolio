About

I'm a PhD-trained UX researcher and service designer with a background in applied linguistics. That training shapes how I work: I pay close attention to how people describe their experiences, where language breaks down, and what goes unsaid.

My work centers on a consistent problem: the systems that most shape people's lives (taxes, government services, healthcare, financial tools) are often harder to navigate than they need to be. I study why that friction exists, what it costs people, and how to design systems that are clearer, more humane, and easier to use.

I'm especially drawn to trauma-informed and non-extractive research practices. In my work, I've built AI-assisted workflows to scale qualitative insight, facilitated alignment across government and engineering teams, and supported designers in strengthening their research practice.
