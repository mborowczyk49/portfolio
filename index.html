01 Govtech · Developer Platform
↳ Stakeholder Interviews (×12) ↳ User Interviews + Concept Testing ↳ Structured Ranking Exercises

Scaling a federal developer platform by surfacing what engineers actually needed

VA's internal developer platform was built on an assumption: templatizing DevOps processes would enable self-service adoption. But adoption was stalling. I led end-to-end research across six engineering teams — pairing stakeholder interviews with structured concept testing — to surface the real blockers: fragmented standards, absent documentation, and no shared spaces for knowledge exchange. The findings directly shaped quarterly OKRs and redirected the platform roadmap.

↗ Shaped quarterly OKRs ↗ Standardized templates prioritized ↗ Office hours redesigned as structured forum ↗ Live demo app with production-ready examples
02 Fintech · Mixed Methods
↳ Observational Sessions (n=22) ↳ Pre-season Survey (n=500) ↳ Tax Peak Survey (n=4,828) ↳ SPSS Descriptive Statistics

Understanding why Credit Karma users abandoned TurboTax — and what rebuilt their trust

For the first time, Credit Karma and TurboTax partnered to offer a joint tax filing experience. I co-led a full tax season research program — observation sessions, two large-scale surveys, and cross-functional synthesis — to understand where users dropped off and why. Key findings: the login wall interrupted exploration before users understood the product's value; redundant authentication flows broke trust; and W-2 photo capture inherited anxiety from prior bad experiences. Every major finding led to a shipped change.

↗ +12% login success rate ↗ +3% W-2 conversion lift ↗ New low-commitment preview experience ↗ Cross-product auth flows redesigned
About

I'm a PhD-trained UX Researcher and Service Designer with 9+ years of experience leading mixed-methods research across government, financial technology, and civic tech. My academic background is in applied linguistics — which shapes everything about how I work: I pay close attention to how people describe their own experiences, where language breaks down, and what's being left unsaid.

I focus on the kinds of problems that sit at the intersection of infrastructure and human experience — internal tools that teams depend on, services that need to work for everyone, workflows that quietly create risk when they fail. I'm particularly interested in how research can be designed to be responsible and inclusive: trauma-informed methods, accessible design, and research practices that don't extract from the communities they're meant to serve.

I've built AI-assisted research workflows that scale qualitative insight, facilitated cross-functional alignment workshops with government and engineering stakeholders, and mentored design teams in research practice. I believe the best research doesn't end with a deck — it ends with a decision.
